My new method called Second Order Thompson Sampling with exponent k in [0,infinity) (you could also make k negative if you want 'negative exploitation', i.e. a higher probability of selecting lower evaluated actions) vary k to select a custom trade-off between exploration vs exploitation

  • k = 1 -> regular Thompson sampling - linear
  • k > 1 -> approaches greedy selection as k approaches infinity (pure exploitation) - superlinear
  • k < 1 -> approaches a uniform sample as k approaches 0 (pure exploration) - sublinear
